# Landing Page Section Library (Direct Response)

Use this as a menu. Most pages should follow:
Hero → Proof → Problem → Solution/How → Benefits → Proof → Pricing → FAQ → Final CTA

---

## HERO VARIANTS

### Hero A: Outcome + Mechanism + CTA (default)
**Headline:** Outcome without pain  
**Subhead:** What it is + who it’s for + why it works  
**Bullets:** 3–5 benefits  
**CTA:** Primary + secondary (watch demo / see examples)

**Notes:** Best for most offers.

---

### Hero B: “From/To” (BAB)
**Headline:** From ___ to ___  
**Subhead:** Bridge statement  
**Visual:** Before/after screenshot, example output, short demo GIF  
**CTA:** Start / Book

---

### Hero C: “Category King” (positioning)
**Headline:** The ___ for ___  
**Subhead:** 1–2 lines on why you’re different  
**Proof:** Logos / stats / 1 testimonial right under

---

### Hero D: “Offer Stack” (services/subscriptions)
**Headline:** Unlimited ___ for $___/mo  
**Subhead:** What’s included + turnaround  
**Bullets:** Scope guardrails (what you do / don’t do)  
**CTA:** Subscribe / Book intro

---

### Hero E: “Problem-first” (PAS)
**Headline:** Stop ___  
**Subhead:** Why it’s happening + what it’s costing  
**CTA:** Get the fix

---

## PROOF BLOCKS (use early)

### Proof 1: Testimonial Strip (3-up)
- 3 short quotes
- Name + role + org
- Outcome mentioned (not “great service”)

**Placement:** Right under hero.

---

### Proof 2: Logo Bar
- “Trusted by” (only if true)
- Use grayscale logos
- Add small qualifier: “Teams at…”

---

### Proof 3: Metric Tiles
3–4 tiles:
- “X% increase in ___”
- “$___ raised / saved”
- “___ hours saved”
- “___ projects delivered”

**Rule:** Never invent numbers. If unknown, use “Typical outcomes” + note.

---

### Proof 4: Case Study Card
- Context: who they are
- Problem
- What you did
- Result (metric)
- Screenshot / artifact

---

### Proof 5: “As Seen In” (only if true)
Keep small; don’t lead with it unless your buyers care.

---

## PROBLEM / AGITATION SECTIONS

### Problem A: Cost of Inaction
- 3–5 bullets of hidden costs
- Make it tangible: time, money, stress, opportunity

### Problem B: “What you’ve tried”
- List common failed solutions
- Explain why they fail (without dunking unfairly)

---

## SOLUTION / HOW IT WORKS

### How it works (3 steps)
1. Step
2. Step
3. Step

**Tip:** Add time expectations (“takes 5 minutes”).

### Unique mechanism block
- Name your method
- 3 pillars
- Why it’s different than alternatives

---

## BENEFITS / FEATURES

### Benefit grid (2x3)
Each card:
- Benefit headline (outcome)
- 1 sentence explanation
- Feature support line (optional)

### Feature table (if technical)
- Feature | What it does | Why it matters

---

## PRICING TABLES

### Pricing A: Single price (Designjoy-style)
- Price
- “What you get”
- “How it works”
- Guardrails
- CTA
- FAQ right under

### Pricing B: 3-tier SaaS
- Most popular middle tier
- Clear difference in *outcomes/limits*
- Include “who it’s for” per tier

### Pricing C: Service packages
- Deliverables list
- Timeline
- What success looks like
- CTA = Book strategy call

### Pricing Add-ons block
- Optional add-ons (keep short)
- Avoid burying core offer

---

## RISK REVERSAL

### Options
- Cancel anytime (subscriptions)
- Trial
- Guarantee (only if feasible)
- “First deliverable free” (if your model supports)

**Microcopy examples**
- “Cancel in 2 clicks.”
- “No long-term contracts.”
- “You approve before anything goes live.”

---

## COMPARISON TABLES

### You vs Alternatives
Columns:
- You
- Hiring
- Agency
- DIY/tools

Rows:
- Speed
- Cost
- Quality control
- Flexibility
- Risk

**Rule:** Be fair; credibility beats dunking.

---

## FAQ SECTION (objection-driven)

Order FAQs by:
1) Price / ROI
2) Time / turnaround
3) Fit / niches
4) Process
5) Risk / cancellation
6) Technical / security (if relevant)

FAQ answer style:
- 2–4 sentences max
- Specific details > reassurance

---

## FINAL CTA SECTION

### Final CTA A: Summary + Button
- Repeat promise
- 3 bullets
- CTA + microcopy

### Final CTA B: “Last proof”
- One strong testimonial
- CTA right below
