# Current Project

## What we are building
The Ainslie Agency website — a lead-generating site that positions us as the go-to GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) specialists. The site needs to educate prospects on what GEO is, why it matters now, and convert them into booked calls or inquiries. This is not a generic marketing agency site — it's a specialist landing experience for a new category.

## What good looks like
- A site that generates inbound leads from businesses who want to show up in AI search results
- Clear, compelling messaging that makes GEO feel urgent and accessible (not academic)
- Strong calls-to-action that drive prospects to book a discovery call or request an audit
- Content that demonstrates our own GEO expertise (practice what we preach)
- Professional enough to justify $2K–$12K/month pricing without feeling corporate or bloated
- Fast, clean, and optimized — the site itself should be a showcase of GEO best practices

## What to avoid
- **Generic/templated feel** — Do not look like every other "digital marketing agency" site. No stock photo grids, no vague "we drive results" copy. We are GEO specialists, not generalists.
- **Over-promising or hype** — No "guaranteed #1 rankings" language. Use real data and specifics. Credibility and trust matter more than hype. Let the market stats speak for themselves.
- **Overcomplicating things** — This is a new agency. Keep the site lean and focused. No unnecessary pages, complex animations, or features we don't need yet. Ship something sharp, iterate later.
- **Ignoring our own GEO** — The site must follow GEO best practices (schema markup, llms.txt, AI crawler access, citability). We can't sell GEO if our own site doesn't do it.
- **Jargon without context** — Prospects may not know what GEO is. Explain it simply before going deep.
